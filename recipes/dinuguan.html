Recipe for Dinuguan

Dinuguan is a traditional Filipino dish made from pork and pig's blood, often served with steamed rice or puto (rice cakes). It has a rich, savory flavor and is known for its unique taste and texture.

Ingredients

  • 10 ounces pork blood
  • 1 lb pork belly, diced
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2-3 green chili peppers, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil

Steps

  1. In a pot, heat the cooking oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic; saut√© until fragrant.
  3. Add the diced pork belly; cook until browned.
  4. Pour in the vinegar and let it simmer for a few minutes without stirring.
  5. Add the water, fish sauce, and sliced chili peppers; stir well.
  6. Pour in the pork blood and stir gently to combine.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Cover and simmer on low heat for about 30-40 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  9. Adjust seasoning if necessary and cook until the sauce thickens.
  10. Serve hot with steamed rice.
  11. Enjoy your Dinuguan!

Recipe for Kare Kare

Kare Kare is a traditional Filipino stew made with oxtail, tripe, and vegetables in a rich peanut sauce. It is often served with bagoong (fermented shrimp paste) and steamed rice. This dish is known for its unique flavor and creamy texture, making it a favorite in Filipino cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb oxtail, cut into pieces
  • 1/2 lb tripe, cut into pieces
  • 1/2 cup peanut butter
  • 1/2 cup bagoong (fermented shrimp paste)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 cup green beans, trimmed
  • 1/2 cup eggplant, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Kare Kare mix

Steps

  1. In a large pot, heat the cooking oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic; saut√© until fragrant.
  3. Add the oxtail and tripe; cook until browned on all sides.
  4. Pour in the water and bring to a boil; reduce heat and simmer for about 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the meat is tender.
  5. Once the meat is tender, add the peanut butter and stir until well combined.
  6. Add the bagoong and stir to incorporate.
  7. Add the green beans and eggplant; cook until the vegetables are tender.
  8. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  9. If using Kare Kare mix, add it according to package instructions and stir well.
  10. Let it simmer for a few more minutes until everything is heated through.
  11. Serve hot with steamed rice and extra bagoong on the side.
  12. Enjoy your Kare Kare!
